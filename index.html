How to play

Bead 12 · 12 Guti
1

The goal

Each player starts with 12 beads. Capture your opponent's beads by jumping over them. The player who takes all the opponent's beads — or leaves them with no legal move — wins.

2

The board & lines

Beads sit on the points where lines cross. A bead may only travel along a drawn line. Every point connects up, down, left and right — but diagonals exist only where a diagonal line is drawn, so not every point has them.

Lines you can travel along
3

Moving

On your turn, slide one of your beads one step along a line to a touching empty point. That's a full turn.

Step to an empty neighbour
4

Capturing

If an opponent's bead sits next to yours along a line, and the point directly beyond it is empty, you jump over it and remove it — just like draughts/checkers.

Jump over, land beyond, remove it
5

Chained captures

After a capture, if the same bead can immediately jump another, it keeps going — chain as many as you can in one turn.

6

Capture rule setting

Optional: capturing is your choice. Forced: if a capture is available, you must take one. Pick whichever you prefer below the board.
